Kuripes & Tepis: Sacred Pipes

What are Kuripes & Tepis?

Kuripes and Tepis are sacred shamanic tools used in traditional Rapé ceremonies. These beautifully crafted pipes serve as the vessel through which the sacred tobacco medicine is administered.

Kuripe (Self-Administration)

A V-shaped pipe used for self-administering Rapé. One end goes into the nostril, while the other end is blown into by the user. This allows for personal communion with the medicine.

Tepi (Serving Others)

A straight pipe used by a practitioner to serve Rapé to another person. This creates a sacred exchange and allows for guided administration of the medicine.

Sacred Craftsmanship

Our pipes are ethically sourced and handcrafted by indigenous artisans, made from sustainable bamboo, bone, or wood. Each piece carries the intention and blessing of its creator.
