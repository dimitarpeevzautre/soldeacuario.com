The Sacred Medicines

Honoring the traditions and spiritual context of each plant ally.

Kambô: The Fire of Transformation

Originating from the Amazon jungle, Kambô is a powerful medicine that cleanses on all levels. It is not a psychedelic but a deep physical and energetic purgative that strengthens the immune system, clears 'panema' (bad luck or negative energy), and brings clarity and stamina.

Preparation (Dieta):

  • Fast for at least 8 hours before the ceremony.
  • Avoid alcohol and recreational drugs for 3 days prior.
  • Stay hydrated, but do not drink water 2 hours before application.

Tabaco & Rapé: The Spirit's Breath

Sacred Tobacco (Mapacho) is a fundamental plant in many indigenous traditions, used for cleansing, prayer, and protection. Rapé, a fine powder made from this tobacco and other medicinal plants, is blown into the nostrils to clear the mind and energy channels, centering the self and connecting to higher consciousness.

Guidelines for Respect:

  • Approach with a clear intention and prayer.
  • Receive with humility and an open heart.
  • Understand this is medicine, not a recreational substance.

The Heartbeat of the Ceremony

Medicine music guides the journey, carrying our prayers and weaving the fabric of the sacred space.

Icaros: Songs of the Plants

These are sacred songs taught directly by the plant spirits. Each icaro carries a specific energy for healing, protection, or calling in allies. Their melodies are the sonic signature of the medicine.

Heartbeat Drum Songs

The steady rhythm of the drum grounds us, connects us to the heartbeat of the Earth, and can guide us through intense parts of the journey. These songs are prayers for strength and courage.

Tools of the Ceremony

Each item holds a sacred purpose, aiding in healing and spiritual connection.

Kuripes & Tepis

Pipes for self-administering (Kuripe) or serving (Tepi) Rapé. They are the sacred instruments used to deliver the medicine's breath.

Ceremonial Drum

The voice of the Great Spirit. The drum's vibration helps to move energy, induce trance states, and call in protective spirits.

Chakapa

A rattle made of bundled leaves, used to cleanse a person's energetic field with a rhythmic, rustling sound.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is the 'dieta' and why is it important?

The dieta is a crucial part of the preparation. It involves abstaining from certain foods (like red meat, pork, spicy food, salt, sugar, and fermented foods) and activities to purify your body and mind. This allows the medicines to work more deeply and effectively, and shows your respect and commitment to the process.

Are the ceremonies safe?

Your safety is our highest priority. Our facilitators are highly experienced and have trained for years with these medicines. We conduct thorough health screenings beforehand to ensure our retreats are suitable for you. We maintain a high facilitator-to-participant ratio to provide individual attention and support throughout the ceremonies.

What kind of accommodation is provided?

Accommodation is in comfortable, shared rooms within our retreat center, separated by gender. This fosters a sense of community. A limited number of private rooms may be available for an additional cost; please inquire when you book.
